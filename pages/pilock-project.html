Pi-Lock System

The Pi-Lock system is an innovative capstone project that enhances the security, access control, and management of the Mac Laboratory at CSPC. With the integration of RFID technology, scheduled access authentication, and real-time monitoring, it provides a modern and efficient solution to the challenges of traditional laboratory management. This would allow only students and faculty members who have scheduled classes to enter the laboratory at a specific time, thus improving security and efficiency in the operations of the system. Besides access control, Pi-Lock provides an all-around attendance tracking, log details, and performance monitoring of connected devices, making it a versatile tool for managing laboratory activities.

Key Features

  •  RFID-Based Access Control:  Users tap their school IDs on RFID scanners to gain entry or exit, ensuring only authorized personnel access the laboratory.
  •  Scheduled Access Management:  Access is restricted based on predefined schedules, allowing only students and faculty with assigned classes to enter during specific times.
  •  Attendance Tracking and Logs:  Automatically records attendance and entry/exit logs, providing detailed data for monitoring and reporting.
